The Philosophy

Obstacles vs.
challenges.

"Things are only obstacles if you see them as obstacles. If you shift your perspective and see them as challenges, then they are something you can overcome."

That single sentence is the operating principle behind every quest below. Fear, to me, is just a different kind of fuel, and sequence matters: the discipline built training for a mountain is the same discipline that builds a company, phase by phase.

Signature Quests

The ones that turned the framework.

Thirty-three challenges live in the full log: physical, extreme, endurance, travel, civic, and personal. Here are the ones I return to most when I tell the story.

2023 · Extreme

Swim with sharks. No cage. Years after cage diving with great whites, he faced the fear head-on, no cage. "Way more like fish than I ever expected. Truly beautiful."

2022 · Endurance · Civic

10K open-water swim. A distance swim undertaken as both a personal and civic challenge.

2020 · Civic · Speaking

TEDx Talk. "Thriving despite Autism, Dyslexia, Processing Disorders & Poverty": the quest that took the framework public.

2018 · Physical

500lb deadlift, five times. At 50. A bodybuilding habit set in 1986, still paying dividends three decades later.

2016 · Travel · Extreme

Trek across the Sahara Desert. One of several multi-week endurance treks across punishing terrain.

2014 · Extreme

Learn to be a bullfighter. One of the purest tests of the "obstacles vs. challenges" principle.

2013 · Extreme

Swim with a great white shark. The quest that would later demand a sequel: without the cage.

1999 · Extreme · Physical

Climb the face of El Capitan. One of the defining physical quests of my twenties.

1986 · Physical

Place top three in a bodybuilding competition. The first documented quest: the one that started everything.

More From the Log

Twenty-four more,
told in brief.

The complete quest log spans six categories and thirty-eight years. A selection from the rest: BASE jumping (2021) · giving one gallon of blood in a year (2019) · a 525-mile bike ride along the California coast (2019) · a San Diego Marathon finish (2017) · a 30-day water fast (2015) · driving an Indy race car and a bobsled at 70mph (2012) · wrestling a crocodile and swimming with a seal (2011) · flying a glider over Lake Tahoe (2005) · running 28 miles across the Sierra Nevada in one day (2002) · driving the Pan-American Highway (2001) · climbing all of California's 14,000ft peaks in 14 days (1997) · and backpacking the John Muir Trail, twice.

Personal milestones run alongside the physical ones: becoming a father three times (1993, 2004, 2006) and meeting President Barack Obama (2018) both sit in the same log, under the same rule.
