The Reframe

Different.
Not deficient.

For most of my childhood, the word that followed me was stupid. The word that should have followed me was different. I was bullied through school, held back, moved through four continuation schools. None of the labels were correct — and none of my teachers had the language for what was actually happening: seven undiagnosed neurodivergent conditions quietly running an operating system the school system was not built to read.

The reframe he argues for now is simple: neurodivergence is not a sliding scale from "less able" to "more able." It is a different operating system. Different inputs, different processing, different outputs. Pattern recognition that overwhelms a classroom can build a category-defining company. The work is not to "fix" anyone — it's to build the rooms, the schools, and the companies the different operating system can actually run in.

The Seven

Seven conditions. One person.

Each has its own architecture — its own strengths, its own friction. No single condition explains the work; the architecture is the combination of all seven, layered and running daily for over fifty years.

Autism Spectrum

A different way of processing social, sensory, and emotional input. Pattern recognition runs hot; small talk runs cold. Strong on systems, depth, and focus.

Dyslexia

A difference in how the brain processes written language — often producing a more visual, more associative thinker on the other end.

Savant Syndrome

An exceptional ability in one domain — the part of the brain that sees frameworks in chaos.

Synesthesia

The crossing of normally-separate senses. Building a company is, quite literally, a multimodal experience.

Sensory Processing

The nervous system handles incoming input differently. What's background noise to most people can be a foreground signal here.

Auditory Processing

Sound is received clearly but processed on a different track. Written communication often lands faster than spoken.

ADHD

A different attention architecture — sustained focus on what fascinates, immediate disengagement from what doesn't.

Together, a System

No single condition explains the work. It's the combination of all seven, integrated and running for over fifty years.
